What Things Can I Do With My Baby?

The bottom line: 

  • Try to limit your infant’s germ exposure in the first few months of their life and lower the risk of illness as much as you can.

  • If you have not had a baby before, it is very difficult to evaluate what it will be like when they are here. Try to be realistic when making plans.

How Should I Care for My Baby’s Umbilical Cord?

The bottom line: The umbilical cord stump will naturally dry up and fall off within 2-3 weeks after birth. Until then, keep it dry, clean, and exposed to air to prevent infection.

What to Expect at Your Baby’s Well Visits (0 to 18 Months)

The bottom line: The first year includes six or seven well visits focused on making sure your baby is growing and thriving, checking your mental health, and reassuring you that while parenting is hard, it’s hard to do wrong.
